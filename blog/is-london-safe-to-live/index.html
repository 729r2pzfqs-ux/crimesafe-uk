Is London Safe to Live? A Complete 2026 Guide

Moving to London? Here's everything you need to know about crime rates, safe neighbourhoods, and what to expect.

Quick Answer: Yes, London is generally safe to live in. While it has higher crime rates than rural areas, most crime is concentrated in specific areas and times. With awareness and smart choices, millions live safely in London every day.

London Crime Statistics Overview

London, home to over 9 million people, naturally has more total crime than smaller cities. However, when you look at crime per capita, many London boroughs are surprisingly safe.

According to the latest Metropolitan Police data:

  • Most common crime: Theft and handling (includes pickpocketing)
  • Violent crime is concentrated in specific areas
  • Residential burglary has decreased over the past 5 years
  • Most crimes occur in transport hubs and nightlife areas

Safest Boroughs to Live In

Based on our analysis of police data, these are the safest London boroughs:

1. Richmond upon Thames - Leafy, suburban feel with excellent schools and parks. Lowest crime rate in London.
2. Kingston upon Thames - Great shopping, riverside location, family-friendly atmosphere.
3. Sutton - Affordable outer London borough with low crime and good transport links.
4. Bromley - Large borough with diverse neighbourhoods, generally very safe.
5. Harrow - Multicultural area with strong community feel and low crime rates.

Areas to Be Cautious About

Some areas have higher crime rates, but this doesn't mean they're "unsafe." Higher crime often correlates with:

  • Major transport hubs (Victoria, King's Cross, Liverpool Street)
  • Tourist hotspots (Westminster, Camden)
  • Nightlife areas (Soho, Shoreditch)

Most crime in these areas is theft and pickpocketing, not violent crime. Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure.

Safety Tips for Living in London

  1. Research your neighbourhood - Use our street crime map to check specific areas.
  2. Be aware on public transport - Keep bags closed and phones secure, especially in crowds.
  3. Secure your home - Good locks and security lights deter burglars.
  4. Know your neighbours - Strong communities have lower crime.
  5. Stay in well-lit areas at night - Stick to main roads when walking late.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is London safe to live in 2026?

Yes, London is generally safe. Most areas have low crime rates, and millions of people live safely here. Like any major city, some neighbourhoods are safer than others.

What are the safest areas to live in London?

The safest boroughs include Richmond upon Thames, Kingston upon Thames, Sutton, Bromley, and Harrow. These outer London areas consistently have the lowest crime rates.

What areas of London should I avoid?

No areas need to be completely avoided, but be more cautious around major transport hubs, tourist areas, and nightlife spots - mainly for pickpocketing. Residential areas are generally safe.

The Bottom Line

London is a safe city to live in. While it has crime like any major global city, the vast majority of residents never experience serious crime. Choose your neighbourhood wisely, stay aware of your surroundings, and you'll enjoy everything this incredible city has to offer.
